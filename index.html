Computational Astrophysics

I study how supermassive black holes interact with the galaxies that host them. My work focuses on AGN outflows—how these fast winds originate, how they propagate through their surroundings, and how they influence the life cycle of galaxies. Using a mix of spectroscopy and data-driven modeling, I trace these feedback processes across scales.

In parallel, I use CMC-COSMIC to model the dynamical evolution of dense stellar clusters, examining how parameters such as binary fraction and black hole retention shape their long-term structure and behavior. More broadly, I work at the intersection of observational analysis and theoretical modeling to understand how energy from black holes regulates star formation, drives gas dynamics, and sculpts galaxy evolution over cosmic time.

Research

AGN Outflows

Analyzing galactic-scale winds driven by supermassive black holes using KCWI spectroscopy to understand AGN feedback mechanisms.

with Prof. Alison Coil

Globular Cluster Dynamics

Simulating N-body dynamics in dense stellar environments to study black hole retention and binary evolution in globular clusters.

with Prof. Kyle Kremer

Dark Matter Detection

Characterizing backgrounds in the XENONnT detector to improve sensitivity for WIMP dark matter searches.

with Prof. Kaixuan Ni

ML for Particle Physics

Developing machine learning models for jet classification and particle identification in high-energy physics experiments.

with Prof. Javier Duarte

KCWI/KCRM Reduction

Processing integral field spectroscopy data from the Keck Cosmic Web Imager, including flux calibration, sky subtraction, and cube mosaicking.

with Prof. Alison Coil
